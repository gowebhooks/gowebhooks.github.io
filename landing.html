Use cases



Why would you want to send out webhooks? Webhooks notify 3rd parties when events occur on your platform, kind of like a push notification. Usually this event is asynchronous so you must pro-actively ping your customers so they can take action.





Fintech Platform

Use webhooks to notify your partners when their customers' invoices are paid, bank transfers are done pending, a new billing cycle is initiated, or when there is an account update.

ML Labeling & Human Processing

Use webhooks to notify your customers when their images are labeled, copy is translated and transcribed, data is cleaned and processed, etc.

Risk Assessment Platform

Use webhooks to notify your customers when your risk team has determined whether their customers or employees are reputable.

Chat Platform

Use webhooks so your customers can notify their end users when a user's status changes or a message is sent.

Analytics Platform

Use webhooks to notify your customers when there are significant events in their data such as when a goal is met or if there is an anomaly in their data.

Partner Platform/Marketplace

Use webhooks to so partners can receive real time information in order to build 3rd party applications on your platform.

Setting up infrastructure to deliver webhooks can get complicated

• You need to spin up separate, asynchronous message queues so your main systems are not stalled while waiting on external webhook delivery.
• External endpoints will often hang or error, leaving your system to deal with retries, notifying and disabling naughty endpoints. In the worse case you can DDoS your own customers or overwhelm your own system.
• Webhooks must be secure, reliable & idempotent. At scale.
• If your team does not have expertise running webhook systems, mistakes can cost your business thousands.
• You need a dashboard to track your deliveries and investigate failures.
